## 4. Master Persona — Cashflow-Clarity Claire

### 4.1 Business profile (the conversion sweet spot)

- Revenue: **$30k–$250k / month** ($300k–$3M / year)
- Headcount: **3–30** employees or contractors
- Already has: business bank account, bookkeeper or QBO, payroll software, Stripe/Square/Shopify, a CPA seen mostly at tax season, maybe Ramp/Mercury/Brex
- Does **not** have: a full-time finance hire, a recurring monthly moment where someone explains the numbers in plain English

### 4.2 The 5-layer emotional stack

| Layer | What she says / feels |
|---|---|
| **Surface problem** | "I need better reporting." |
| **Real problem** | "I do not trust myself to make the next financial decision." |
| **Private fear** | "I'm growing a business that looks successful but is financially weaker than I realize." |
| **Emotional desire** | "I want someone serious looking at this with me." |
| **Identity desire** | "I want to operate like a real CEO, not someone duct-taping the finances together." |

### 4.3 Ideal industries

Agencies · Clinics · Trades businesses · Professional services · Small consulting firms · E-commerce operators · Restaurants & hospitality · Creative studios · Small law / accounting / advisory firms · Local service companies.

### 4.4 Disqualifiers — the site must respectfully repel these

- Pre-revenue founders
- Hobby businesses, very early solopreneurs
- Owners who only want free templates and nothing more
- Businesses that need full bookkeeping / tax cleanup before any analysis is possible
- Companies that already have a CFO or full FP&A function
- Owners unwilling to review financials monthly

Hard qualification protects premium conversion. The "Who It's Not For" section is **not optional**.

### 4.5 Internal-dialogue bank — Claire's verbatim thoughts *before* landing

Use these as raw material for hero, headlines, ad copy, email subjects:

- "Revenue is up, but why does cash still feel tight?"
- "I have QuickBooks, but I don't actually know what to do with the reports."
- "My bookkeeper keeps the records clean, but I still feel like I'm guessing."
- "Can I afford to hire?"
- "Should I cut expenses?"
- "Are we actually profitable, or does it just feel that way?"
- "I know I need to look at the numbers more often, but I never have time."
- "I don't want to hire a CFO yet, but I need something more serious than this."
- "Why does revenue look good but the business still feels fragile?"
- "Do I actually know what's happening, or am I just checking the bank account and hoping?"
- "I need to look at the numbers, but I don't want to open QuickBooks."
- "Is my business too small for this?"
- "Is my financial data safe?"
- "Will I still need my bookkeeper?"

## 5. Awareness Stages & Belief Chain

### 5.1 Awareness map (the site must convert all five)

1. **Unaware** — she's stressed but hasn't named the problem.
2. **Problem-aware** — "My finances are messy."
3. **Solution-aware** — "I should hire someone or use a tool."
4. **Product-aware** — "Is the Monthly Finance Desk worth $1,500/mo?"
5. **Most-aware** — ready to apply.

### 5.2 The belief chain (in order)

1. **"They understand my real problem."** → pain-recognition hero.
2. **"This is not another spreadsheet/dashboard."** → category-gap subhead.
3. **"I can experience this before I commit."** → interactive hero demo.
4. **"This is for someone like me."** → industry chips + qualification copy.
5. **"I can try it safely."** → "no bank connection required" microcopy.
6. **"This is worth $1,500/month."** → value-stack + CFO comparison.
7. **"I should apply."** → application CTA.

### 5.3 The hero must trigger 5 emotions, in order

**Recognition → Discomfort → Curiosity → Safety → Desire.**

If a hero variant skips Discomfort or Safety, it does not ship.

> **Executive clarity for small business owners.**
> Calm. Premium. Plain-spoken. Slightly serious. Never hype.

Think: a private memo from a senior banker to a portfolio company owner — not a SaaS landing page.

## 9. Visual System — Decisions Filter

### 9.1 What the site must feel like

- A private finance room
- A premium SaaS product
- An executive reporting environment
- A high-trust financial command center
- A serious operating system for owner-led businesses

### 9.2 What the site must NOT feel like

- A bookkeeping app
- A cheap spreadsheet download site
- A generic accounting dashboard
- A casual small-business tool
- A technical/devtool AI product
- A tax-season-only advisory service

### 9.3 Palette

| Role | Token direction |
|---|---|
| Background | Deep charcoal / near-black (`hsl(220 18% 5%)` family) |
| Foreground | Warm off-white, never pure `#fff` |
| Accent (primary) | Champagne / muted gold (`hsl(40 55% 70%)` family) for trust + premium feel |
| Accent (secondary) | Deep institutional green for "financial steadiness" moments |
| Accent (rare) | Restrained electric blue **only** for AI/data states (sample briefing, prompt focus) |
| Borders | Soft warm gray, 1px, low opacity |

**Forbidden:** purple/indigo gradients, neon, candy colors, pure black on pure white, default Tailwind palette out-of-the-box, dark-mode-toggle UI.

### 9.4 Type

- Headlines: serif-influenced display or premium grotesk (e.g., a refined sans with editorial weight). Tracking tight on display sizes.
- Body: clean modern sans, 16–17px, generous line height.
- Figures in the briefing card: tabular numerals; monospaced acceptable.
- **Forbidden:** Inter as the only typeface, Poppins, playful rounded fonts, Comic-anything, Google "AI default" pairings.

### 9.5 Imagery & components

**Use:** product UI mockups, briefing memo previews, spreadsheet fragments, glass-like cards, thin grid lines, abstract data textures.

**Forbidden:** stock photos of people pointing at laptops, cartoon finance icons, hand-drawn doodles, 3D coins/dollar-bill clipart, isometric SaaS illustrations.

### 9.6 Motion

Calm, slow, intentional. ≤300ms easing on UI; 600–1000ms on hero reveals. No loading theatrics. No jittery counters. No parallax orbs. No mouse-trail effects.

## 11. Application Qualification Logic — 3 Steps

The site qualifies hard. The CTA is **Apply**, never **Buy**.

**Step 1 — Contact & basics**
- Name · Business name · Email · Monthly revenue range · "What are you trying to understand about your finances?"

**Step 2 — Business context**
- Industry · Team size · Current bookkeeping / accounting setup · Biggest financial frustration · Currently using spreadsheets?

**Step 3 — Fit**
- Comfortable with $1,500/month? (yes/no)
- Willing to review financials monthly? (yes/no)
- Have a business bank account you can connect via Plaid? (yes/no)

A "no" on any Step 3 question routes to the free template library, not to a rejection page.

## 12. The Decisions Filter — 12 Questions

Before any copy block, section, color, CTA, component, motion, or page ships, the agent must answer **YES** to all 12. One NO = rework before ship.

1. Does it speak to Claire's **real problem** (interpretation), not her surface problem (more data)?
2. Does it use **approved vocabulary** (Section 6.2) and avoid every **forbidden phrase** (Section 6.3)?
3. Does it feel like a **private finance room**, not a generic SaaS landing page?
4. Does it respect the **four-world positioning** — not bookkeeping, not dashboard, not CFO, not template store?
5. Does it use **calm, premium motion and visuals** — no hype, no orbs, no confetti, no neon?
6. Does it preserve the **locked hero spec** (Section 7) if it touches the hero?
7. Does it preserve the **locked homepage section order** (Section 8) if it touches structure?
8. Does it route **low-intent visitors to free templates** and **high-intent visitors to the application**?
9. Does it **avoid overclaiming Goldman Sachs affiliation** in public copy?
10. Does it advance Claire through at least one step of the **belief chain** (Section 5.2)?
11. Does it **qualify hard** — respectfully repelling non-fit buyers instead of pretending to serve everyone?
12. Does it answer at least one objection from the **objection bank** (Section 10)?

If a change does not touch a given dimension (e.g. a pure color tweak), mark that question N/A — but never skip the filter entirely.
