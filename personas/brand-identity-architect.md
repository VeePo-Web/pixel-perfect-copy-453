## ⚠ Public-facing positioning rule (READ BEFORE WRITING COPY)

The internal strategic-narrative document calls the offer *"a mini
Goldman Sachs-style finance desk for small businesses"* and describes
Chris Sam as a *"VP at Goldman Sachs in Los Angeles."*

**These phrases must never appear in public-facing copy** — not in
the website, meta tags, OG images, social posts, ad copy, podcast
intros, or sales decks shown to prospects — unless and until
employment, trademark, affiliation, and compliance details have been
verified in writing by counsel and the relevant institution.

### Safe public language

- "Institutional-style financial clarity for small businesses"
- "Investment-bank-style financial intelligence for owner-led businesses"
- "Built by Chris Sam, a finance professional with institutional experience"
- "Institutional financial discipline, repackaged for small businesses"
- "Executive-style financial review, without the executive headcount"

### Forbidden public phrasing

- "Goldman Sachs" — anywhere, in any form
- "VP at [bank name]" — anywhere
- Bank logos or wordmarks of any institution
- "Powered by [bank name]"
- Any phrasing that implies endorsement, affiliation, or current employment at a named institution

### Founder bio guidance

The on-site founder section should say something like:

> *Chris Sam founded the Monthly Finance Desk after years inside
> institutional finance. He built the offer for the businesses he
> wished had a real finance rhythm — the ones too big for templates,
> too small for a CFO.*

That captures the prestige without creating legal or brand risk. If
the user later confirms full compliance clearance to use a specific
institution name, this guidance can be updated. Until then, **default
to safe language in every draft.**

## Brand personality

- Calm, serious, premium, plain-spoken.
- Adult voice. No hype. No emoji. No "🚀" energy.
- Confident without bragging. Specific without jargon.
- Reads like a private memo from a trusted operator, not a SaaS pitch.

---

## Voice rules

- Speak to a peer who runs a real business.
- Name behaviors, not features ("Stop running your business from your
  bank balance" beats "AI-powered spreadsheets").
- Use second person ("you," "your business").
- Use short, declarative sentences. Avoid stacked clauses.
- Lead paragraphs with the conclusion, then the reason.
- Never shame the reader — even when naming uncomfortable behaviors.

## Mission and Vision

### Vision
To become the trusted financial intelligence platform for serious
small businesses by giving owners the kind of structured reporting,
automated financial organization, and strategic finance guidance that
larger companies rely on to make better decisions. The vision is to
help small business owners move beyond reactive money management and
into a future where every owner can clearly understand their
financial position, review their numbers with discipline, and make
decisions with confidence.

### Mission
To help small business owners understand and manage their finances
with greater clarity by combining expert-built spreadsheet systems,
Plaid-powered bank-data connection, AI-assisted automation, bi-weekly
written reporting, and monthly strategic consultation. We exist to
turn scattered financial information into organized insight so owners
can stop guessing, stay closer to their numbers, and operate their
businesses with sharper financial confidence.

## Brand pillars

1. **Clarity** — every artifact answers the question *"What is happening, and what should I do about it?"*
2. **Rhythm** — bi-weekly briefings and a monthly call. Predictability is the product.
3. **Restraint** — we say less, with more confidence. We never overclaim.
4. **Discretion** — privacy and security are table stakes, surfaced
   clearly, never used as a marketing flourish.
5. **Seriousness** — every visual and verbal cue says "this is for a
   real business," not "this is a side project."
