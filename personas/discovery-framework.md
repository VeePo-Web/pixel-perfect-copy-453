# Discovery / Application Framework

**DECISION-MAKING REFERENCE ONLY. Does not render anything.**

How to structure the application flow, the monthly call, and onboarding.
Premium signal without interrogation.

---

## Rules

- Never start with a long questionnaire.
- Never ask for bank access in the application.
- Never request financial documents upfront.
- Never make the user feel interrogated.
- Apply in under 3 minutes total.

---

## Application steps

### Step 1 — Fit check

- Name
- Email
- Business name
- Monthly revenue range (chips, not free text):
  - Under $30K/mo (likely not a fit — surface free templates)
  - $30K–$75K/mo
  - $75K–$150K/mo
  - $150K–$250K/mo
  - $250K+/mo (route to a different intake)
- What are you trying to understand about your numbers? (short text)
- CTA: *Continue to Business Context*

### Step 2 — Business context

- Industry (chips: Agency, Clinic, Trades, Restaurant, Consultant, E-commerce, Local Service, Professional Firm, Other)
- Team size (chips: 1–3, 4–10, 11–20, 21–30, 30+)
- Current bookkeeping/accounting setup (QuickBooks + bookkeeper, QuickBooks alone, Xero, spreadsheets only, CPA only, none)
- Biggest financial frustration (short text)
- Currently using spreadsheets? (yes/no)
- CTA: *Continue to Fit Review*

### Step 3 — Readiness

- Comfortable with $1,500/month? (yes / maybe / no)
- Willing to review financials in a monthly one-hour call? (yes/no)
- What would make this valuable for you? (short text)
- CTA: *Submit Application*

### Confirmation

> *Your application has been received. If the Monthly Finance Desk
> looks like a fit, we'll follow up with next steps.*

No "you're in" energy. Premium signal comes from selectivity.

---

## Disqualification handling

Don't reject hard. Route soft.

- Under $30K/mo or "no" to $1,500 → *We don't think the Monthly
  Finance Desk is the right fit yet. Start with the free template
  library and apply again when the business has grown.*
- "No" to monthly review → *The Monthly Finance Desk depends on a
  recurring conversation. If that isn't a fit, the free templates may
  still help.*

---

## What the monthly call actually does

The monthly one-hour consultation is **the rhythm**, not a sales call.
Frame it internally as the owner's *finance room* or *monthly
financial boardroom* — the scheduled moment they step out of daily
operations and look at the business from a higher financial perspective.

### Agenda (the call's repeatable structure)

1. **Last month in review** (10 min) — Walk through the two most
   recent bi-weekly briefings. What changed? What got better, what
   got worse?
2. **Surface questions** (10 min) — Pull the "Questions for Monthly
   Call" line items from the briefings. Discuss each.
3. **Next 30–60 day decisions** (25 min) — Hiring? Pricing change?
   New software? Cash reserve top-up? Tax planning? Owner draw? Talk
   through each with the numbers in view.
4. **Action list** (10 min) — Three to five concrete actions the
   owner will take before the next call. Documented.
5. **Wrap** (5 min) — Confirm next call, flag anything to watch in
   the next two briefings.

### What the call is NOT

- Not a sales pitch.
- Not a deck.
- Not theatre.
- Not bookkeeping cleanup (that's the bookkeeper's job).
- Not tax filing (that's the CPA's job).

### What the owner walks away with

A short written summary delivered within 24 hours: decisions reviewed,
actions agreed, items to watch in the next two briefings.

## Onboarding rhythm (post-acceptance)

Typically 7–14 days from accepted application to first briefing.

| Day      | Step                                                                                  |
| -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Day 0    | Application accepted. Welcome email + onboarding checklist sent.                      |
| Day 1–2  | 30-minute kickoff call. Map existing setup (QuickBooks, bookkeeper, bank accounts, payroll, software). |
| Day 3–4  | Plaid connection of business bank accounts. Read-only, tokenized.                     |
| Day 5–7  | Seed the spreadsheet system with the owner's historical data (last 90 days minimum).  |
| Day 7–10 | AI-assisted categorization + manual review. Owner approves chart of accounts mapping. |
| Day 10–14 | First bi-weekly briefing delivered. First monthly call scheduled for ~Day 30.        |

---

## Discovery call (between application and acceptance)

If the application reads green, the first call is a 30-minute fit
conversation, not a sales pitch.

### Agenda

1. **What is the business doing right now?** (5 min) — Stage, revenue,
   team, what's working, what isn't.
2. **What does the current finance setup look like?** (10 min) — Tools,
   people, frequency of review, biggest gap.
3. **What decisions are coming up in the next 90 days?** (10 min) —
   Hiring, pricing, expansion, fundraising, tax planning.
4. **How the Monthly Finance Desk would handle those.** (5 min) —
   Specific to her situation, not a generic walkthrough.

End with: *"We'll follow up by end of day with whether we think this
is a fit, and how onboarding would work."*
