# Footer Architecture

**DECISION-MAKING REFERENCE ONLY. Does not render anything.**

---

## Job

The footer is a serious closer, not a sitemap dump. It must reinforce
two things: *trust* (privacy/security/founder) and *one last action*.

---

## Structure (left to right, 4 columns desktop, stacked mobile)

### Column 1 — Brand

- Wordmark "Monthly Finance Desk."
- One-line positioning: *More than bookkeeping. Lighter than a full CFO.*
- Founder line: *Built by Chris Sam.*

### Column 2 — Product

- How It Works
- What You Get
- Sample Report
- Pricing
- FAQ

### Column 3 — Free templates (lead capture)

- Cash Flow Forecast Template
- Monthly Financial Review Template
- Hiring Affordability Calculator
- Owner Dashboard Template
- All Templates →

### Column 4 — Trust

- **Privacy & Data Handling** (full page, not modal)
- **Security** (how Plaid is used, what is stored, where)
- **Terms**
- Contact email (real address, no contact form)

---

## Bottom bar

- Left: `© [year] Monthly Finance Desk. All rights reserved.`
- Right: Final CTA — *Apply for the Monthly Finance Desk* (text link in champagne, not a button — the button lives above).

---

## What never goes in the footer

- Social icons (no Instagram, Twitter, TikTok, LinkedIn badges in the
  footer — if a LinkedIn link is needed for Chris, it lives inside
  the founder section on the homepage, single text link).
- "As seen in" logos unless verified.
- Newsletter signup (the template library is the lead capture).
- Trustpilot / G2 widgets until reviews exist.

---

## Privacy treatment

Privacy is **not** a footer afterthought. The link is bold, full-color,
and the destination page explains:

1. What is required for the hero preview (nothing sensitive).
2. What is required after onboarding (Plaid bank-data connection).
3. How Plaid works (read-only, tokenized, never store credentials).
4. What we do and do not do with financial data.
5. Where data is stored and who has access.
6. Deletion and offboarding policy.

Do not overclaim security certifications unless confirmed. Use precise,
truthful language.
