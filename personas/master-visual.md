## Three core filters (every decision passes through these)

### 1. Elevate human experience

- **Principle**: Empathy first. Research, inclusivity, emotion, trust.
- **For the Monthly Finance Desk**: We serve owners during one of the
  most emotionally loaded subjects in their business — money. Every
  pixel must honor that anxiety with calm authority, never shame.

### 2. Embody brand truth

- **Principle**: A website is the brand made tangible. Cohesive system,
  longevity over trend.
- **For the Monthly Finance Desk**: Calm, premium, institutional-style
  financial clarity. Every element should feel like a private financial
  operating room — serious, precise, restrained. Not a startup toy. Not
  a bookkeeping dashboard. Not an accounting template store.

### 3. Innovate responsibly

- **Principle**: Creativity with purpose. Tech serves the experience.
- **For the Monthly Finance Desk**: AI and Plaid are *supporting
  mechanisms*, not the primary transformation. Never lead with "AI
  magic." Lead with the rhythm and the interpretation.

---

## North star aesthetic

A calm financial command center.

- Dark, premium, spacious, editorial.
- Glass-like report cards.
- Restrained motion.
- Plain-English interpretation surfaced, not hidden behind charts.

---

## What it must never look like

- Generic bright SaaS blue gradient hero.
- Cartoon coins, playful finance illustrations, clip-art charts.
- Fake Wall Street imagery (stock tickers, candlestick charts, bull statues).
- Confetti, spinning loaders, "AI sparkle" animations.
- Dense spreadsheet-style typography on marketing pages.

---

## The emotional test

After 10 seconds on the page, the visitor should think:
*"This feels serious enough for my business."*

If a design choice does not contribute to that feeling, cut it.
